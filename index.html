I'm a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Radiology at UNC-Chapel Hill (with Profs. Hongtu Zhu & Tengfei Li), after a Ph.D. in Statistics at Sun Yat-Sen University. I work where biostatistics, machine learning, and large-scale health data meet.

Right now I'm building AI models for electronic health records that bring together clinical events, genetics, imaging and text to get a clear picture of each patient: their risk, when a disease starts, and how conditions add up. Earlier work covers statistics for high-dimensional data (JASA, Statistica Sinica), medical-imaging AI, and COVID-19 modeling.

Off the clock you'll find me on a court with a racket in hand, or keeping an eye on the Pokémon wandering along the bottom of this page. Good science, like a good rally, is about patience, timing, and the occasional bold winner.

Multimodal EHR foundation models, medication-aware longitudinal analysis, and reproducible lupus / autoimmune pipelines across UK Biobank, All of Us & OMOP clinical data.

High-dimensional Statistics
